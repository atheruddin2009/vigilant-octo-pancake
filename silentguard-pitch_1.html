01 / Problem

Panic buttons fail exactly when you need them most.

Every safety app on the market assumes the same impossible thing: that in the middle of an emergency, you'll have a free hand, a clear head, and the time to open an app.

0 sec

The time you actually have to fumble for your phone during an assault, a fall, or a struggle.

SOS

Existing apps rely entirely on a manual trigger — one press, correctly aimed, under panic.

Alone

Solo commuters, delivery workers, elderly residents — no one watching, nothing watching back.

02 / Solution

An AI that listens for distress, and acts without being asked.

SilentGuard runs quietly in the background, reading audio and motion patterns on-device — and only speaks up when something is genuinely wrong.

STEP 01

Listen

Mic and motion sensors run a lightweight on-device model — always on, nothing recorded by default.

STEP 02

Detect

Fuses audio + motion signals — a scream, a fall, a struggle — to flag distress with very low false positives.

STEP 03

Confirm

A short grace period lets you cancel with a gesture or safe-word before anything is sent.

STEP 04

Alert

Live location + audio snippet auto-shared with contacts, via app, SMS fallback, or local helpline.

Zero-action safety

No button, no unlocking, no app to open — protection that's already running before you need it.

Privacy by design

On-device detection only — audio is analyzed, never streamed or stored, unless a real alert fires.

Multi-signal accuracy

Audio + motion fusion cuts false alarms far below single-signal safety apps.

Works anywhere

SMS fallback means it functions with poor connectivity and on low-end phones.

Grace-period control

You stay in control — a simple cancel window keeps the system trustworthy, not trigger-happy.

Built-in distribution

Deployable fleet-wide by universities, cab platforms, and employers as a safety-compliance layer.
